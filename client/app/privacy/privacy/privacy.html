Privacy Policy

Proquo is an interactive online platform located at www.proquo.com.au, and any related software or application, which allows businesses to sell, purchase and swap services (the "Platform"). In order for us to provide the Platform, we need to handle Personal Information provided by you. We take your privacy seriously.

This policy (the "Privacy Policy") tells you how we collect, handle and use your Personal Information. Please read it carefully before using our Platform. The Privacy Policy should be read in conjunction with the Proquo Terms and Conditions located at www.proquo.com.au/terms. We comply with the Privacy Act as a matter of best practice.

This Privacy Policy may change. If we change our Privacy Policy, we will display notice of changes on our Platform. Please check for updates regularly.

If you do not agree to the terms of this Privacy Policy or any updates, you should stop using the Platform. Otherwise, by using our Platform, you accept this Privacy Policy.

Definitions

So that our Privacy Policy is easy to read, we have defined some more terms to keep them short:

  • When we say "we"., "our", "Proquo" or "us", we mean Proquo Pty Ltd ACN 612 292 010, our officers, shareholders, employees, contractors, agents, licensees and other representatives.
  • When we say "you", "your" or "User", we mean you – the user of our Platform, whether you use the Platform as a registered Member or not.
  • When we say the "Privacy Act", we mean the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) as amended by the Privacy Amendment Act 2000 (Cth) and the Privacy Amendment (Enhancing Privacy Protection) Act 2012 (Cth).
  • When we say "Personal Information", we mean that term to include any information or opinion, whether true or not, about a person who is identified or whose identity can reasonably be established, and not limited to information in a material form.

We also use some of the same definitions as listed in the Proquo T&Cs such as "Member Accounts", "Member Profiles", "Briefs", "Quotes" "Member Contract", "Payment Provider" and "Members". Please refer to the definition section of our Proquo T&Cs.

By continuing to use the Platform, you hereby consent to our collection, use and disclosure of your Personal Information for the purposes specified in this Privacy Policy.

2. Supply of Personal Information

Before supplying us with any Personal Information, you should ensure that there are no restrictions on you doing so (whether under contract, under the Privacy Act or otherwise). We are not responsible for any mistake or negligence or other conduct on your part where you supply Personal Information which you are not entitled to supply, but we will take reasonable steps to destroy or de-identify any such wrongfully supplied Personal Information within a reasonable time after you have given us notice.

3. How do we collect Personal Information from Users?

We collect Personal Information where reasonably necessary for the proper function of the Platform. The ways in which we may collect Personal Information include:

  • When we receive enquiries or correspondence via the Platform;
  • When you register a Member Account via the Platform;
  • Through the use of browser 'cookies' or trackers;
  • When you communicate with other Users via the Platform;
  • When you enter into transactions via the Platform;
  • From third-party payment services, when details of your transactions with other Users via the Platform are provided to us.

The kinds of Personal Information we may collect include your legal business name, ABN, business logo, your occupation and the kind of business you run, your phone number, your date of birth, your business address, your bank or credit card details, your personal and email addresses, your photograph or image and geographical information. When you browse or use our Platform, we may collect non-personal statistical information, in accordance with clause 15 below. Additionally, we may collect information that has not been directly solicited from you, and is reasonably necessary for the proper function of the Platform. Where such unsolicited information falls outside this reasonable necessity, we will endeavour to destroy and/or de-identify the information as soon as practicable. This clause does not exhaustively list the ways in which we may collect Personal Information or the kinds of Personal Information we may collect. We or our Payment Provider reserve the right to request further proof of identification before disbursing any money to you for a transaction.

We will not ask you to provide Personal Information about your racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, philosophical beliefs, sexual orientation, criminal record or membership of a trade union, or your health, genetic or biometric information ("Sensitive Information"), unless it is reasonably necessary. If you do provide us with Sensitive Information, for example by including it in your profile on the Platform, and it is not reasonably necessary for us to have that Sensitive Information, we will destroy the Sensitive Information or ensure that it is de-identified.

4. What Personal Information is publicly available?

Your Member Profile will be publicly available to all Internet users whether or not they are registered as a Member on the Platform.

Where a Member transacts with your via the Platform, that Member will have the opportunity to review and 'rate' your Services ("Reviews"). The Reviews will also be publicly available to all Internet users and may include your Personal Information. If you believe the content of a Review is inaccurate, inappropriate or otherwise objectionable, you may flag the Review for our attention by using the button next to each Review which will open a new email addressed to support@proquo.com.au.

Communications between you and:

  • Us will be kept private; and
  • Other Users via instant messaging on the Platform will be private between you and that User, although we will be able to see the contents of those communications and we may share those communications with our Payment Provider (and other necessary third parties) where necessary to resolve a support or technical issue or a dispute.

5. What kind of credit card and bank account information do we collect?

We (or our Payment Provider) may request, store and use credit card, bank account and other billing information provided by you. If you are paying for services, you will need to provide the name of the credit card holder, the credit card number, expiry date and applicable CCV code. If you are receiving money for services, you will need to provide bank account details for the account that is receiving payment, including BSB, account number, billing address and account name. When paying for and receiving services via the Platform, you may also be required to provide other credit or bank account information, at our request. It is very important that all credit card and billing information is accurate and up-to- date. We may ask you to prove that you are the genuine cardholder or bank account holder, in our discretion.

6. Can you be anonymous or use a pseudonym (false name)?

You are not allowed to give us a pseudonym or false name when you register as a Member. This is very important to maintain the integrity of the Platform and protect other Users. You need to be signed in as a Member to make the most of the Platform, and to start selling, purchasing or swapping services. When you are signed in, you are not anonymous to us or to other Users.

7. How do we use your Personal Information once we have collected it?

We may use or disclose Personal Information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To identify you and authenticate your identity;
  • To enable you to create your Member Account and Member Profile, post Briefs on your Member Profile, supply Quotes, enter into Member Contracts, interact with other Users via the Platform, and otherwise use our Platform;
  • To enable you to sell, purchase and swap services on the Platform;
  • To customise the content of our Platform so that it matches your preferences and the kinds of businesses and services you may be interested in;
  • To understand usage trends and patterns and improve your overall experience of the Platform;
  • To fix problems and respond to feedback and complaints;
  • To enforce any agreements between you and us;
  • To enable a third party (e.g. our payment services provider) to provide you with goods or services on our behalf;
  • To provide dispute resolution services to you and other Users in relation to any Member Contract or other disputes;
  • To allow us to run our business and perform administrative and operational tasks such as training staff, risk management, software development, maintaining a database, management our relationships with Payment Processors and undertaking research, planning and analysis; and
  • To contact you about the Platform or for direct marketing purposes (see paragraphs 9 and 10 below).

We may share and license non-personal statistical data to third parties including advertising or analytics entities. We will not share or license any such information that identifies you personally, and endeavour to aggregate this data in such a way as will de-identify individual users. In addition, the Privacy Act sets down some particular situations called Permitted General Situations where your Personal Information may be disclosed outside of the purposes listed above. "Permitted General Situations" include where we need to take action in relation to unlawful activity relating to our functions, to establish or defend a legal claim, to lessen or prevent a serious threat to public health or safety, or to assist in locating missing people. Other laws, or a court, might also legally compel us to disclose Personal Information.

We store and make back-ups of certain Personal Information to meet our tax and reporting and other government and administrative obligations, and to ensure that we are able to recover information if our systems experience a fault or outage.

Apart from the situations discussed at paragraph 7 above, we will contact you by email with workflow-related matters associated with your Projects in Progress, and otherwise when it is reasonably necessary: for example, when a Member is urgently trying to contact you regarding a transaction, when we need to discuss a payment, when you are in dispute with another Member or when we have important information about your Member Account.

To assist us, please ensure you provide us with your correct details, and let us know if you believe the information we have about you is inaccurate, incomplete, out-of- date or misleading. 15. Do we use cookies, trackers and analytics?

We may use 'cookies' to store data on your computer. The information stored in the cookie is used to identify your browser or device. This enables us to operate an efficient service, to track the patterns of behaviour of Users of the Platform and to collect statistical information about visitors to the Website and/or the Application, including but not limited to IP addresses, geographical location, how you arrived at the Website and information about the device being used to access the Services such as the browser or operating system.

We collect this information so that we can improve our Platform, direct the most appropriate content to you and remember your preferences for next time you sign in. You can modify your browser to prevent cookie use but if you do this, our Platform may not work properly. In addition to our use of cookies, third-party advertisers or ad servers may also place or recognise cookies on your browser in the course of serving advertisements to the Platform. The use of cookies by any such third-party advertiser or tracker is not subject to this Privacy Policy, but subject to the privacy policy of the relevant entity.. This data is collected through cookie technology. You may opt out of this collection by installing relevant browser extensions.
